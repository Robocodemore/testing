10. How to use Slack

Slack housekeeping and general guidelines:

In general, the instructors are here from time to time, but they do not moderate Slack. Instructors are busy, creating and updating content, sourcing speakers, and focused growing this nanodegree in and outside of the classroom. If you want to ask an instructor a question, you can post the question in the #officehours channel and on Tuesday's from 11:00 am - 12:00 pm PST one of the instructors will be answering questions.

You are welcome to follow all of the channels, but we recommend that you follow the channels that relate to your current place in the program.

How to join a new Slack channel:

You may join multiple Slack channels. To join a new channel:

  1. Click on "Channels" on the left-side panel of your Slack window in order to scroll through all channels or search for a channel name.

3..Click on the green "Join Channel" button at the bottom of the channel preview.

Program Slack Channels

Depending on what you are looking for, there are a few slack channels you can join. From finding fellow students, to project support, to job postings, there is a lot of useful conversation happening in slack.

Below we've listed some of the channels you might be interested in!

Term channels

These channels are mainly for term specific announcements and news.

Project Channels

#Project 1 - 8, are designated for project related questions and discussion
Simply, join when you are working on the project and leave when you are done.
